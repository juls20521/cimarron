Hollis Elias Crane

Title: Deputy Sheriff, Doña Ana County

Subject: Deputy Hollis Crane. Status: Active Law Enforcement.

Identification & Status

  • Name: Hollis Elias Crane
  • Title: Deputy Sheriff
  • Hometown: Boston, Massachusetts
  • Age: 38
  • Relationship Status: Widowed
  • Face Claim: Aaron Poole

Appearance

A wiry man with dark eyes that often squint in a calculating manner. Maintains a strictly professional appearance: well-pressed coat, polished boots, and a hat kept meticulously free of dust.

Personality

  • Polite & Calm: Never raises his voice, even when delivering threats.
  • Controlling: Obsessed with order and the "civilization" of the frontier.
  • Calculating: Prefers legal maneuvers and financial ruin over physical violence.
  • Ambitious: Focused on vertical movement within the territorial government.
  • Fondness for Strays: Known to secretly feed mangy mutts behind the county jail.

Motivations

Desires: Command of respect (or fear) and the power to impose total order on the chaotic frontier.

Secrets: Currently on the payroll of the Sierra Madre Mining & Exploration Company. Continues to write weekly letters to his deceased wife, Eleanor.

Fears: Chaos; situations where he lacks bureaucratic or physical control; public humiliation.

Biography

1836: Hollis Elias Crane is born in Boston, Massachusetts, the son of a successful merchant. He is raised with the belief that a well-ordered society and personal prosperity are inseparable.

Late 1850s: He completes his education, becoming a young and ambitious lawyer in Boston, focused on corporate and financial law.

1861-1865: During the Civil War, Hollis avoids military service. Instead, he uses his legal skills to work for the Union war effort, managing contracts and supply logistics. This experience confirms his belief that bureaucracy and manipulation of the law are the true sources of power.

1866: After the war, he joins the Pinkerton Detective Agency, seeing it as a way to enforce order and use his skills in a new capacity.

1867: He meets and marries Eleanor, a kind and intelligent woman who sees past his cold ambition.

1869: While investigating a volatile labor dispute, Hollis fails to prevent a violent riot. His wife, Eleanor, is killed in the chaos. This event completely hardens him, as he blames his own "softness" for her death.

1870: Desperate for revenge and justice, Hollis falsifies evidence to secure a conviction against the riot's ringleader. The public scandal that follows leads to his quiet but humiliating dismissal from the Pinkerton Agency.

1871: Hollis leaves Boston and heads west, seeking a remote place where his past is unknown. He views the frontier as a chaotic landscape ripe for a man who knows how to control it.

1872: He arrives in the New Mexico Territory and is deputized by an overstretched and corrupt Doña Ana County Sheriff.

1874: He begins to align himself with the newly arrived Sierra Madre Mining & Exploration Company, seeing them as the future of the region and the path to his own lasting power and wealth. Though alliances may just shift with the arrival of the Cimarron Railroad Company to the area.
