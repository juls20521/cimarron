Elma Jones

Current Alias: Isabel Hart

Subject: Isabel Hart (Alias), recently arrived at CRC camp as a mail-order bride.

Basic Information

  • Real Name: Elma Jones
  • Current Alias: Isabel Hart
  • Past Aliases: Grace, Mary, Catherine, Mary Wells
  • Hometown: Columbus, Ohio
  • Occupation: Nanny, Seamstress (currently posing as a stranded mail-order bride)
  • Age: 23
  • Relationship Status: Unwed (engaged 3 times)

Appearance

Red hair, often carefully curled. Respectable and feminine dress with care taken to appear modest. Face claim: Conor Leslie

Personality

  • Adaptable: She becomes whoever her audience needs her to be: the helpless damsel, the grateful servant, the demure bride-to-be.
  • Calculating: Every tear, every trembling smile, every confession is strategic while appearing utterly guileless.
  • Self-Preserving: Years of survival have taught her that no one else will look after her.
  • Guarded: Beneath all her personas, the real Elma Jones remains locked away.
  • Lonely: Late at night, when the mask slips, there's a hollowness she won't acknowledge.

Motivations & Disposition

Desires: To recover the money, bonds, and jewelry that Charlie Mills took from her in Texas; Financial security.

Secrets: Her real name; Multiple matrimonial cons; Theft from the Clement family in Texas.

Fears: Being recognized; Ending up destitute and powerless; That she may be incapable of genuine love.

Biography

1851: Born in Ohio. Father departed early; mother struggled alone.

1859: Mother succumbed to consumption. Elma lived with an aunt for 6 months before being placed in an overcrowded orphanage.

1861-1865: Orphanage conditions deteriorated during the war; food became scarce and discipline harsh.

1866: Placed on an orphan train at 15. Taken in by a farming family in Illinois. Worked dawn to dusk as a servant, learning the art of manipulation by watching those around her.

1869: Ran away at 18. Found work as a maid, but found the wages bleak.

1870: Her first con. Courted a shopkeeper, accepted a proposal, and left town with his household funds. "It had been almost too easy."

1871-1874: Perfected her technique across Missouri (Grace), Kansas (Catherine), and Texas (Mary).

1874: As "Mary Wells," she worked as a nanny for the Clement family in Texas. In July, she stole cash, bonds, and jewelry from their safe. However, Charlie Mills witnessed her departure. She handed over the loot to buy his silence.

Nov 1874: Having tracked Mills to New Mexico via the Frontier Index, she arrived at the Cimarron Railroad Company camp as "Isabel Hart," posing as a mail-order bride to get close to her target.
