Cash McCoy

Outlaw

Subject: Cash McCoy

Basic Information

  • Character Name: Caspian McCoy
  • Nickname: Cash, The Cherokee Kid, The Last McCoy
  • Hometown: Born in Westport, Missouri. Grew up just over the border in Kansas.
  • Occupation: Card player / Outlaw
  • Age: 25
  • Relationship Status: Unwed

Appearance

Tanned skin, shaggy blonde hair, and a crooked nose. Face claim: Owen Wilson

Personality

  • Silver-Tongued: Cash can talk his way into a high-stakes game (and once out of a hanging!)
  • Perceptive: He has a strong ability to read people, noticing the slight sweat on a brow or the twitch of a finger.
  • Amiable: If people are laughing at his jokes, they aren't looking for the extra card up his sleeve.
  • Risk-Addicted: He thrives on the rush and feels most alive when the odds are stacked heavily against him.
  • Morally Flexible: He’ll steal from a greedy railroad tycoon without a second thought, though he’d never take a dime from a widow.
  • Rootless: He views any form of permanent attachment as a cage he can't afford to be in.

Motivations & Disposition

Desires: Stay richer than the next outlaw, find the men who abandoned him and Hank and give them a "McCoy welcome" to the afterlife.

Secrets: During the fatal confrontation over the disputed cattle brand, it was actually 16-year-old Cash (not Hank) who killed the local land agent in a panic. Hank took the blame. Cash has let everyone (including family) believe Hank was the killer.

Fears: One of his brothers survived the war and later became a lawman. Cash fears a confrontation with him someday.

Biography

1850: Born in Westport, Missouri, the youngest of five children.

1854: The McCoy family relocated to Kansas Territory the moment it opens for settlement. They staked a claim near the Kansas River, just over the border, but the dream of a peaceful ranch was quickly overshadowed by the violent Bleeding Kansas border conflicts.

1861-1865: The two oldest McCoy brothers are drafted. Too young to fight, Cash mostly remembers the war as a series of raids. Both Jayhawkers and Bushwhackers steal cattle and horses from the McCoy ranch. Cash learns early on that "causes" are just excuses for men to take what isn't theirs.

1866: Following a fatal confrontation over a disputed cattle brand, Cash’s older brother Hank killed a local land agent. (See secret). Blinded by loyalty, 16-year-old Cash helped Hank escape, fleeing south into the lawless Indian Territory (to the Creek Nation). Here they get mixed up with an outlaw gang.

1869: A botched heist turns deadly. Hank is killed by a posse after their "friends" leave them high and dry. Cash barely escaped by abandoning his horse and disappearing into the brush.

1870-1874: Cash drifted through boomtowns, mining camps, and cattle towns, refining his skills as a cardsharp and starts telling the pretty girls with the slightest rebellious streak that he had a Cherokee grandfather to impress them. He gained the nickname "The Cherokee Kid." He moved from Kansas to Texas and finally toward the territories further west.

1875: Following rumors of easy money, Cash arrives at the Cimarron Railroad Company camp in New Mexico Territory. The railroad camp is the perfect blend of high-stakes opportunity and a way to stay ahead of the law.
