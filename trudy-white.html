Biography

November 5, 1840: Born as Gertrude Ross in Ohio

1859: Married Abraham White, a man of moderate wealth and standing

June 8, 1861: Has son - Oliver

1862: Abe joins the war effort on the Union side.

1863: Receives a letter that Abe is officially MIA. He never returns.

1864: Begins writing. Some of her stories get picked up by local newspapers.

1869: Needing a change, Trudy takes Ollie and heads west for a job as a reporter.

1871: Hollis leaves Boston and heads west, seeking a remote place where his past is unknown. He views the frontier as a chaotic landscape ripe for a man who knows how to control it.

1870: Spring. Two children (siblings) arrive in town to live with their uncle. But when their uncle fails to meet them, Trudy takes them into her care for "just the night."

1870: Early summer. Days turn into weeks and weeks into months when it becomes known that the uncle has passed and there is no response to letters to their mother.

1870: Late summer. After a favor from a friend, the children are legally transferred to her care until a living relative can be located.
