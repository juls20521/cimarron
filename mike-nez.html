Mike Nez

Occupation: Bounty hunter, occasional scout, opportunistic trader/thief

Mike Nez (Ma'ii / Ashkii). On the trail of a bounty.

Basic Information

  • Character Name: Mike Nez
  • Nicknames: Mike, Ma'ii, Ashkii
  • Hometown: Born near Canyon de Chelly, Navajo Nation (New Mexico Territory / modern day Arizona)
  • Occupation: Bounty hunter, occasional scout, opportunistic trader/thief
  • Age: 35
  • Gender: Male
  • Relationship Status: Widower / Currently Unwed
  • Face Claim: Chaske Spencer

Appearance

Lean with weathered features that make him appear older than his years. Dark hair typically kept shorter than traditional Navajo style, practical for his work. Dresses in a mix of Army surplus, Navajo, and civilian clothes - whatever serves his purpose.

Personality

  • Pragmatic: Michael presents different faces to different people - respectful and compliant around authority figures when it serves him, charming and trustworthy when making deals, cold and efficient when hunting bounties.
  • Observant: He's able to read people and situations quickly
  • Principled: While ruthless in pursuit of survival and profit, he maintains certain moral lines - he won't harm children or elders, and feels some loyalty to other displaced Native people.
  • Emotionally Guarded: Wary of deep attachment and a cynical view of human nature.
  • Dark Humor: His humor tends toward the sarcastic and dark.

Desires

Wealth: To accumulate enough money and resources to never again be at the mercy of others' decisions about his fate.

A Worthy Legacy: He wants his actions, even the harsh ones, to ultimately mean something – protect someone, right a small wrong, leave a mark that isn't just a bounty poster or a stolen item.

Secrets

Silent Mercy: He sometimes takes bounty jobs specifically to warn his targets if they're Native people, letting them escape while claiming they evaded capture.

A Hidden Token: He carries a flower blossom made of turquoise hidden in his gear that belonged to his wife.

Fears

Being trapped: or confined again like at Bosque Redondo, losing his independence and ability to move freely.

Skin-walkers: Dreads the yee naaldlooshii not as myth, but as a manifestation of the spiritual corruption witnessed at Bosque Redondo, leaving him vulnerable.

Biography

1839 - Born near Canyon de Chelly (New Mexico Territory / modern day Arizona) - May 1.

1853 - 1864 - Becomes a capable warrior and hunter, gaining skills with both traditional weapons and firearms.

1862 - Married

1864 - First son born.

1864 - Long Walk. Soldiers burn his home, kill his livestock, and his people are marched 300 miles to internment at Bosque Redondo (near Fort Sumner). The journey first claims his infant son and, not long after, his wife.

1864 - 1868 - The four years of imprisonment broke something fundamental in Ma'ii's worldview - he had believed in the strength of his people and the protection of traditional ways, but Bosque Redondo taught him that survival required adaptation and pragmatism above all else. He takes the name 'Michael', learns English more fluently, and advocates for his people - all while being criticized by other Navajo for his cooperation with authorities.

1868 - a treaty allowed the Navajo to return home, but Michael found himself unable to settle back into traditional life. The deaths of his wife and child haunted him, and his willingness to work with whites during imprisonment had earned him suspicion from some in his community.

1869 - Enlists as Army Scout, spending four years tracking hostile bands across New Mexico and Arizona territories.

1873 - Discharged. Civilian life offered few opportunities. Too Indian for most white society, too militarized for traditional Navajo life, he carved out a niche in the shadowy spaces between legal and illegal enterprise, taking up bounty hunting.

Late 1873 - Forms an alliance with Rachel Bradley after they helped each other get away from a group of ne'er-do-wells. They claim to be bounty hunters, but if other opportunities arise, well, he's willing to weigh the risks vs the reward.

1874 - Michael and Rachel head toward the CRC Camp in search of several bounties rumored to be in the area.

RP Events:

  • May 1874 - Mike and Rachel decide to pursue Cole Sawyer, the man responsible for robbing nearly $10,000 from the train last year.
  • June 1874 - Find out that Cole Sawyer has connections to John Adams and Dorothy Edwards, outlaws that have been spotted near Blue Water Texas.
  • July 1874 - After a ruse where Rachel "rescues" Dorothy from being captured by Mike, she portrays herself as a friend to try to find Sawyer. When the ruse starts to slip, a fight follows and the two outlaws are captures. Using some 'persuasive' interrogation, the two outlaws reveal that Sawyer's girlfriend was Sophie Donter. She operates the boarding house at the CRC Camp. Later that night, the outlaws slip their bonds and escape. Mike believes that Rachel set them free. Their short-lived partnership crumbles.
  • September 1874 - Mike arrives at the CRC Camp
