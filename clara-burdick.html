Personality

Clara has an abundance of enthusiasm and a genuine care about others. She’s spontaneous, highly adaptable to change. She's also disorganized and prone to procrastination. Good at dreaming up ideas, but not as good at seeing them through to completion. She has a small mountain of started, but unfinished craft projects.

ENFP

Desires

Clara would very much like to be a writer and to travel the whole country... maybe even the whole world! She likes to write over the top stories of grand adventures. Mostly these are short pieces. She has a few larger stories in the works, but they remain unfinished and fragmented.

Secrets

For the last several weeks, Clara has been helping an outlaw by bringing him food and letting shelter in the barn loft when the coast is clear. She finds the man very charming and harbors some fantasies about him whisking her off into the sunset.

Fears

Heights

Her family finding out she's been aiding an outlaw.

Biography

Born August 13, 1852 in Texas to a ranch foreman. Clarissa is the 2nd of 6 children. She spent the bulk of her childhood on the Austin ranch where she received an education in reading, writing, mathematics, cooking, sewing, horse riding, and even a little bit about cows. During the war, there were fewer men to tend the ranch, which meant the women had to help with some of the tasks outside their usual domain. Still, she is by no means an expert at horses or cattle.

Thankfully, their home was never directly threatened during the war. And her older brother and her father (both named John) both survived the war and life settled back into some semblance of normal when they returned.

Recently, with the coming of the railroad, her father saw the opportunity to own land of his own and has moved the entire family to northern Texas where they acquired 160 acres and started raising cattle. The ranch, named 'Fools Gold' is still getting started, and is not yet successful.

As she’s come of age, Clara has taken more of an interest in the cowboys (and a bit infatuated by the idea of handsome outlaws) than the cows or the horses.
