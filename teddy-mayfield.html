Theodore Mayfield

Occupation: Bounty Hunter

Subject: Theodore "Teddy" Mayfield. Noted Bounty Hunter and Confederate Veteran.

Basic Information

  • Name: Theodore Mayfield
  • Nicknames: Ted, Teddy
  • Hometown: Farmville, VA
  • Age: 31
  • Relationship Status: Married (Estranged)
  • Face Claim: Bruce Campbell

Personality & Disposition

  • Strong belief in Justice: Guilty or innocent, the place to sort it out is a courtroom. He tries to capture rather than kill.
  • Competitive: He doesn't like to lose. Thankfully, in bounty hunting, there's really no such thing as cheating.
  • Reliable: If Teddy gives you his word, he'll keep it. He still sends money home every month.
  • Perceptive: Notices when things change or when someone is acting "off."
  • Careful: Reckless hunters get shot. He stops, looks, and listens before jumping into danger.

Motivations

Desires: Save up $1000 for a solid retirement; become famous for capturing a high-profile criminal.

Secrets: He is a teetotaler but pretends to drink alcohol to put others at ease. He is haunted by an incident in South Texas where he mistakenly shot an innocent man.

Fears: Scorpions (checks his boots every time).

Biography

1842: Born May 2, outside of Farmville, VA to tobacco farmers. One of five children.

1850s: Mentored by his Uncle Silas, a former lawman. Learned to shoot, hunt, and navigate the Virginia canals.

1858: After thieves stole a family tobacco shipment with no legal recourse, Teddy developed a lifelong resentment toward those who steal the labor of others.

1860: Left the farm to work for the Southside Railroad on a track repair crew, beginning his love for the rail lines.

1861-1862: Married Sara Smith after a brief courtship. Became a father to a son, Richard. Shortly after, he was drafted into the 21st Virginia Infantry Regiment.

1865: Emerged from the Civil War a hardened veteran with deadly rifle skills.

1868: Unable to settle into civilian life and feeling like a stranger to his family, he headed west to Texas with a promise to send wages back home.

1869: Found his calling as a bounty hunter after successfully capturing a group of rustlers for a local cattleman.

1873: Following reports of crime waves at the railroad camps in the northern Texas panhandle, Mayfield turned his sights toward the Cimarron Railroad lines.

RP Events

December 25, 1873: Mayfield teams up with the Pinkertons to corner John Adams and Dotty Edwards at the T'áp Trading Post. After the capture of Adams, Mayfield was shot in the back. Edwards is blamed.
