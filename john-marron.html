Personality

While he can be outwardly friendly and charming, ultimately JR is out for himself. People are to be used to reach his goals and if he hurts people along the way he'll try to stifle any sense of guilt about it. When the going gets tough, he prefers to move on so that he never has to actually confront any of his wrongdoings. In close relationships, he can be passive-aggressive, using people’s faults and shortcomings against them as blackmail. He's also lazy, which can sabotage some of his money-making aspirations, and only works when he's running low on money.

Desires

Wealth: He's not good at keeping money, frittering it away like water, so he's constantly on the hunt for more. The easier and quicker, the better.

Secrets

JR has likely fathered several children. And hasn't stuck around for any of them.

Fears

While John tries to act like nothing in the world scares him, he is actually scared of sickness and disease. He avoids folks that cough or even sniffle with allergies. He avoids corpses. He avoids doctors. He's only been terribly ill a couple times in his life and that feeling of not being able to fully function (and somehow being stuck in that state forever) is horrible.

Skills

  • Horses - while he doesn't have a 'gentle touch' with the creatures, he knows how to handle them and how to recognize a high quality animal.
  • Cattle - if you're gonna steal 'em, you'd better know how to herd 'em.
  • Harmonica - mostly self taught. Doesn't really like to play for the benefit of anyone else, but breaks it out once in awhile for his own entertainment.
  • Intimidation - it's all about the confidence. Most people don't want a fight. Capitalize on that.

Biography

Born and raised in Texas to a poor family that had a whole mess of kids. His father worked as a ranch hand and by the time J.R. was 10, he was hired on as well to help bring in more money.

There was always something "not quite right" about him and the boy had a cruel and vicious streak. When he was 11, his father caught him tormenting a calf for no other purpose than amusement. He got a good whippin' for it, but it didn't seem to help much other than to make him hide it better from his father. Still, the rancher they both worked for got on his case several times for being abusive to the animals.

When he was 15, he took an interest in the rancher's 14 year old daughter. But when she wasn't immediately receptive to that interest, he took out his frustrations on one of the ranch's horses, whipping it so terribly that it was permanently blinded in one eye.

There was little doubt in anyone's mind who had done this, and after a heated argument, his father and the rancher both told him in no uncertain terms he was no longer welcome there. He has bounced around from town to town, job to job. Sometimes managing to get himself fired. Sometimes just up and quitting when something pissed him off. Or right after someone in town ended up dead.

There are no official murder charges against him. But there's a few towns he's certainly not too keen on returning to.

Now he finds himself down to just a few dollars and headed to Kansas looking for work. A job with the railroad and the transient nature of the camps suits him just fine...
