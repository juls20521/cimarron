Letitia Blackert-Trousdale

Occupation: (Wife of) Railroad Investor / Stakeholder

Subject: Mrs. Noah Trousdale. Investor and Stakeholder in Cimarron Railroad Co.

Basic Information

  • Character Name: Letitia Blackert-Trousdale
  • Nicknames: Lettie, Black-Widow, Mrs. Trousdale, Mrs. Black
  • Hometown: Charleston, South Carolina
  • Age: 36 (Born June 7, 1834)
  • Gender: Female
  • Relationship Status: Married to Mr. Noah Trousdale
  • Face Claim: Jessica Chastain

Personality

  • Vain: Values her looks and painstakingly ensures that her hair, makeup, clothing and jewelry are nicely put together.
  • Dutiful: Attends to her husband's needs without complaint.
  • Selfish: She can appear caring and generous, but it’s an act. Ultimately, she will act for her own interests.
  • Innovative: She rarely needs to resort to violence (at least not by her own hand) to get her way.
  • Ambitious: Always looking toward the future, diversifying her investments, and expanding her influence.

Motivations & Disposition

Desires: An ownership share in the railroad.

Secrets: She has been having an off and on affair with a man fleeing justice.

Fears: Losing her youth and good looks (and thus her control over the men in her life).

Biography

June 7, 1834: Born in Charleston, South Carolina as the only child of Dolores and Clifton Blackert, a well-to-do banker.

1851: Due to several very bad investments, Clifton Blackert found himself deep in debt. A family friend, Mr. Stewart Gyles (decades her senior), offered to pay the debts in exchange for Letitia's hand in marriage. She dominated the submissive Gyles and enjoyed the status and gifts he provided.

1855: Stewart Gyles catches a fever and passes away at the age of 57. Letitia inherits his small fortune at only 21 years of age.

October 1856: Marries 30-year-old Peter Wells, owner of a gambling establishment. The marriage was marked with violence and jealousy.

August 1857: Sustained a bad cut from a broken glass bottle that left a scar on her right arm.

September 1857: Wells was shot in front of Letitia during a brawl. His murderer was never identified. Letitia inherited and sold the gambling hall immediately.

November 1857: Letitia gives birth to a baby boy, who died in his crib two days later.

1858: Marries 35-year-old Nathaniel Terner, a widower with a daughter. Relocates to Terner’s ranch near Lawrence, Kansas.

August 21, 1863: Terner killed by Quantrill's Raiders. He was found shot alongside an overturned wagon. Letitia inherits the ranch and custody of his 10-year-old daughter, Andrea.

1865: Andrea Terner dies after being thrown from her horse and breaking her neck.

March 1870: Marries Noah Trousdale (60), a wealthy entrepreneur heavily invested in an upcoming railroad project.

April 1870: The couple relocate to the frontier town of Wichita to be closer to the project.

January 1874: Lettie acquires partial ownership in the Cimarron Railroad Company. Her goal is now to gain the controlling interest.
