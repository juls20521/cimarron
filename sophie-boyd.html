Sophia Gayle Donter Boyd

Occupation: Boarding House Proprietress (Cimarron Rail Camp)

Subject: Sophia "Sophie" Boyd. Operator of the camp boarding tent.

“There’s only one place I want to go, and it’s to all the places I’ve never been.”

Basic Information

  • Character Name: Sophia Gayle Donter Boyd
  • Nickname: Sophie, Soap
  • Hometown: Austin, Texas
  • Age: 21 (Born Oct 1, 1853)
  • Relationship Status: Married to John Boyd
  • Face Claim: Taissa Farmiga

Appearance

Long, mousy brown hair that's usually done up to keep it out of her face. She usually wears modest dresses well suited for work. She owns one nicer, but still very modest dress for church on Sundays.

Personality

Though she’s well-intentioned and a hard worker, Sophie often gets into trouble because of her tendency to daydream while doing chores. It's not too uncommon to find her dancing with her broom or pausing to appreciate interesting shapes in the clouds.

She's friendly with guests that stay at the boarding house. Sometimes too friendly - chattering and engaging in gossip. Your secrets are not safe with her. She's a bit gullible though and is prone to believe any bit of juicy gossip without questioning it too hard.

She has a longing for adventure. She hears tales from travelers and yearns for something more for herself. Something exciting!

Motivations & Disposition

Desires: Adventure and Romance! She has daydreams about getting whisked off into the sunset by a handsome lawman. Or even a dashing outlaw.

Secrets: Sophie has a habit of snooping into people's things. She's never taken anything, but looks in luggage, open diaries, reads letters.

Fears: The dark; things that go 'bump' in the night. Her imagination can get the better of her sometimes. If it's a really dark night, she'll leave the lantern burning low - just in case.

Biography

1853: Born October 1, 1853. She is the middle of 5 children. Walter (1844), Alice (1849), Clifford (1854), Hugh (1859). Her parents ran a boarding house, so there was a constant stream of new folks to meet. Some stayed for only a night or two. Others stayed for months, or even a year. Sophia sporadically attended a 1 room schoolhouse where she learned how to read and write and some basic arithmetic, though she never did conquer long division.

1862: Walter conscripted into the war effort. Funds get tight at home. Alice and Sophie both had to stop attending school.

1864: Sophie's father falls ill, further stressing the family's finances. Alice is frequently sent away to cook and clean for others while Sophie helps out more with the boarding house chores and duties.

1865: The end of the war brings a short period of chaos and near lawlessness to Austin. The boarding house is occupied for several months by a group of rather unscrupulous men that pay well. The youngest of the bunch, a 16-year-old named Silas, tells wild tales of excitement and adventure to 12-year-old Sophie and 11-year-old Clifford. They hatch a private plan to run away with these men and live a life of adventure. It was not to be though. As they arrived with their bags packed, Silas laughed and sent them back home, much to Sophie's humiliation

1866: Father passes away.

1868: Alice marries.

Spring 1871:Sophie's never quite forgotten that call for adventure. As she approaches 18, she hatches a plan that will win her mother's blessing to leave home. She sees an ad calling for workers for the CRC. She fakes a suitor (that she's dubbed Silas) and exchanges several letters with him - only writing his letters to her in case anyone should demand to see them.

Summer 1871: Surprise, surprise, Silas proposes marriage (a ruse of course). Sophie makes her plans to travel once she turns 18 in a couple months.

Fall 1871: Walter requests his friend, William Bohannon, who was headed toward the rail line, escort his little sister. Making them both promise to send word when they've arrived safely. She already has a plan to invest her meager savings into a boarding house when her fiance isn't there to meet her.

RP Events

  • 1871 - A drifter named John Boyd helps Sophie set up her boarding house. They agree that he'll help her with physical labor in exchange for a cot.
  • June, 1872 - Sophie kidnapped by Charles Bole, which lures John into a trap. The two try to make their escape, but Bole's gang catches up. John stays back to make sure Sophie escapes.
  • June, 1872 - John Boyd proclaimed dead after escaping capture.
  • July, 1872 - A letter from Arthur Ross arrives, raising suspicions that John might just be alive.
  • June, 1873 - Sophie meets Cole Sawyer and the two take a shine to each other.
  • Aug, 1873 - Train robbed by Cole Sawyer and his crew. $10,000 stolen. Sophie suspected of being in cahoots and detained for nearly 2 weeks before being released.
  • Sept, 1873 - Sophie is led into a trap by Thomas Schneider, under the false pretense that she's going to meet Cole Sawyer. Instead, she is grabbed and bound, spending multiple weeks as bait for Cole Sawyer, who never comes.
  • Oct, 1873 - Rescued from captivity by John and Dotty (aka Jay and Anna), they take her to stay with her friend, Birdie Carr.
  • Dec, 1873 - John Boyd reunites with Sophie at the camp's Christmas party.
  • Jan 28, 1874 - Marries John Boyd.
  • April 10, 1874 - Bole's men attempt to kidnap Sophie, but are thwarted by Mr. Bao and a pinkerton agent, Miss Gibson.
  • June 12, 1874 - Sophie and John encounter Charles Bole and his minions at the ghost town of Redemption. Bole is shot dead. Finally, the couple are free of his constant shadow.
