Elmer Benjamin Stroud

Occupation: Prospector/Treasure Hunter

Subject: Elmer "E.B." Stroud. Status: Active.

Basic Information

  • Character Name: Elmer Benjamin Stroud
  • Nicknames: E.B., Elly, Yelmer, Yelly
  • Hometown: Near Raleigh, North Carolina
  • Occupation: Prospector/Treasure Hunter
  • Age: 55
  • Relationship Status: Unwed

Appearance

Mostly gray beard. Blue eyes. Calloused and rough hands. Elmer walks with a noticeable limp, his left leg nearly an inch shorter than his right from an old injury. He wears custom boots with an extra thick sole on the left to help mitigate this. Face Claim: Bill Pullman

Personality

  • He will usually back down from a fight. To the point that most would consider him a coward.
  • Though Elmer is warm, friendly, and caring, taking wholehearted enjoyment in the company of their nearest and dearest, he is, at heart, an introvert and he doesn't mind solitude.
  • Won't swear in front of a woman, though he doesn't mind swearing in front of kids - as long as they are boys.
  • He's nearly always a follower or a loner, and has trouble stepping into any kind of leadership role.
  • Can turn gruff and grouchy when he feels stressed.

Traits

  • Walks with a noticeable limp: This was no war wound, but rather his left leg got nearly crushed when railroad ties fell off a train car back when he was working for the Wilmington and Raleigh Railroad.
  • Strong eyesight: Even if a bit diminished with age, he still has better than 20/20 vision.
  • Hums and sings while he works.

Secrets, Desires, Fears

Secrets: He robbed graves in California back in '55 & '56.

Desires: A wife. He's not too old, dang it! Least not maybe for a nice war widow. Enough wealth to support his wife. North Carolina brightleaf tobacco - the finest in all the world.

Fears: His wealth being stolen (when he finds it, of course). Confrontation and violence.

Biography

October, 1815 - Born in North Carolina as Elmer Benjamin Stroud. His parents both worked for another family that owned a cotton mill.

1834 - 1840: Laborer, Wilmington and Raleigh Railroad.

1838 - Courted Hannah Wilkerson for nearly a year, but she decided to marry someone else.

1840 - Injured, lost job with railroad. Moves in with sister's family and her husband's turkey farm.

1841 - 1849 - Picked up a few odd jobs when he could, mostly helped out on the Reneau farm.

1849 - Still unlucky in love and tired of being a burden on Emily's family, he heads west. One more '49-er'.

March, 1851 - Lucky strike! Finds a sizable gold deposit and makes a small fortune.

April - December 1851 - With newfound wealth comes friends galore! And lady friends! Elmer courts Ida Bradfield, a beautiful woman with expensive tastes. By year's end he's blown through half his money.

1852 - Invests nearly all his remaining wealth into the hunt for more gold. Some is found, enough to keep the operation going for nearly 2 years before finally going broke again.

1854 - Ida moves on to wealthier suitors.

1855-56 - After losing his short-lived fortune, he was at a low point financially and took to grave robbing for a short time, something he is very ashamed of and has never told a soul.

1861 - His injury precludes him from needing to serve in the war, for which he's secretly glad.

1862 - He was rich for a few hours... after working diligently for several weeks at a spot he suspected might prove fruitful, he was rewarded with a nice nugget. But some mudsill robbed him of it and in that moment, Elmer handed it over rather than fight for it.

1862 - 1870 - He's kept at it, slowly & steadily working his way east, sometimes sheltering at a fort for the winters. He finds a bit of gold here and there, always just enough to keep the dream alive of striking it rich again.

1871 - He's close to giving up this prospecting life! His knees hurt. His back hurts. His leg hurts more and more with each year. He'd been working his way to Wichita, planning to take the train east back to Tennessee, but he's heard a few rumors of buried treasure here in Kansas. Just maybe he'll stay a little while - see if he can get to the bottom of these rumors. Pan in the rivers for gold in the meantime. Never know - maybe he'll get lucky.
